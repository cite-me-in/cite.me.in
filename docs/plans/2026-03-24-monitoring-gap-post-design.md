# Blog Post Design: Monitoring Gap

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Title:** Everyone's talking about how to get cited by AI. Nobody's built the speedometer.
**Voice:** Founder talking (personal, direct — not SEO-optimized)
**Publish date:** 2026-03-24

## Structure

1. **Hook** — The GEO/AEO advice machine is running at full speed. Listicles, checklists, agency playbooks. Add schema. Update llms.txt. Write FAQs. But nobody's telling you if any of it is working.

2. **The stakes are real** — AI referral traffic grew 527% YoY in 2025. AI visitors convert at 4.4x the rate of organic. Each AI platform only cites 2–7 domains per response vs Google's 10 blue links — placement is winner-take-most. Link to Yext March 2026 analysis and AI referral traffic stats.

3. **The measurement gap** — If you spent three months on GEO, do you know if your citation rate changed? Which platform? Which queries? Most founders don't. Existing tools are enterprise-priced and report share-of-voice, not whether your domain is getting cited more.

4. **The speedometer** — That's what cite.me.in is. Runs your queries on each platform, tracks every citation, shows you the trend. Name the analogy explicitly — everyone's talking about how to drive faster, we built the speedometer.

5. **CTA** — "Here's what tracking your AI citation footprint actually looks like." → link to sign up.

## Notes

- No FAQ section
- Single CTA at the end
- Link out to studies for data points (Yext March 2026, AI referral traffic stats)
- Part of Track 1 (Monitoring Gap) in the content plan
