# Content & Product Plan: AEO/GEO Narrative

**Goal:** Extend the blog to attract founders and growth teams searching for AI citation visibility. Each post leads to a product feature that delivers on what the post promises. Posts can be interspersed with other topics — this list is the spine, not the full calendar.

**Audience:** Founders and growth teams asking "why isn't AI citing me?" — not SEO practitioners already fluent in GEO/AEO.

**Approach:** Content-led. Write the post first to generate leads and validate interest, then build the corresponding feature.

---

## Track 1 — The Monitoring Gap

### Blog: "Everyone's talking about how to get cited by AI. Nobody's built the speedometer."

**Angle:** GEO/AEO advice is everywhere — write better content, add schema, update your llms.txt. But it's all output with no feedback loop. A founder who spent 3 months on GEO has no idea if their citation rate actually changed.

**Key points:**
- LLMs cite only 2–7 domains per response (vs Google's 10 blue links) — placement is winner-take-most
- AI referral traffic grew 527% YoY in 2025; AI visitors convert at 4.4x the rate of organic
- Without measurement, GEO is just guessing
- cite.me.in is the speedometer: runs your queries on each platform, tracks every citation over time

**CTA:** "Here's what tracking your AI citation footprint actually looks like."

**Research data to cite:** Yext (March 2026 platform behavior), AI referral traffic stats

### Product: Onboarding/messaging reframe

- Update homepage hero to "Search Console for AI" framing
- Add copy about the monitoring gap ("You're running GEO blind")
- Ensure the citations page clearly shows trend data at a glance

---

## Track 2 — The Competition

### Blog: "We looked at every AI visibility tool. Here's the one thing none of them do."

**Angle:** Honest comparison from a founder who built the monitoring layer. Scrunch, Profound, AthenaHQ, Peec AI, Semrush AI Visibility Toolkit — all enterprise-priced, all oriented toward brand safety and share-of-voice reporting. None of them track citation trends over time per platform for an individual domain.

**Key points:**
- What each tool does well (be fair)
- The common gap: no time-series citation data per platform, no query-level detail
- Who each tool is for vs who cite.me.in is for (founder vs enterprise)

**CTA:** Founder-friendly pricing, free to start.

**Tools to cover:** Scrunch, Profound, AthenaHQ, Peec AI, Semrush AI Visibility Toolkit

### Product: Competitive citations view

- On the citations page, show which competitors appear in queries where you *don't* appear (the gap)
- "These queries are citing your competitors but not you" — surfaces lost opportunities
- Extends the existing Top Competitors card (brand names + links already built)

---

## Track 3 — Platform Differences

### Blog: "Why Perplexity cites you and ChatGPT doesn't (or vice versa)."

**Angle:** Each platform has distinct citation behavior based on how it retrieves sources. Founders see this discrepancy on their dashboard and don't know what to make of it. Explain the patterns with real data.

**Key points (from Yext March 2026 research):**
- **Perplexity** — live web search; rewards fresh, crawlable content
- **ChatGPT** — blends training data with search; strong bias toward established domains, Wikipedia (7.8% of citations)
- **Claude** — leans on training data; cites UGC and community content at 2–4x the rate of other models
- **Gemini** — Google-integrated; rewards pages that already rank in traditional search
- Implication: visibility on one platform doesn't transfer automatically

**CTA:** See your per-platform breakdown and understand where your gaps are.

### Product: Per-platform recommendation cards

- On each platform tab in the citations page, add a small "Why [Platform] cites what it cites" card
- Show platform-specific recommendations based on citation patterns
  - e.g. Claude tab: "Claude favors user-generated content — consider adding customer testimonials or case studies"
  - e.g. ChatGPT tab: "ChatGPT strongly favors established domains — third-party coverage (press, directories) increases citation probability"
- Content can be static to start; can be personalized later based on actual citation data

1. **Post 1** (monitoring gap) — fastest to write, no product dependency, sets the narrative
2. **Messaging reframe** — copy update to homepage, low effort
3. **Post 2** (competition) — can publish while competitive feature is in progress

5. **Post 3** (platform differences) — publish when platform feature is close to shipping
6. **Per-platform recommendation cards** — ships with or shortly after Post 3

Other blog posts can be interspersed anywhere in this sequence.
